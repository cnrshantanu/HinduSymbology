Shiva Symbology

Snow capped mountains: Purity of mind.

Third eye: Eye of wisdom and burning of desires.

Half Closed Eyes: Balance between material and spiritual indulgence.

Open Hand: Attainment of wisdom.

Water Pot (Kamandalu): Breakaway from attachment to the physical world and cleansing of egoistic desires

Meditative Pose: Attainment of self realization.

Ganga: Purification and removing of ignorance.

3 Matted Locks of Hair: Integration of physical, mental and spiritual energies (ideal of yoga).

Trident (Trishula): Destruction of evil and ignorance with will (iccha), action (kriya) and knowledge (gyana).

Drum (Damaru): Taming of the restless mind and 2 states of existence (manifest-unmanifest)

Tiger Skin: Potential energy.

Cobra Coiled Around Neck: Transcending Present, Past and Future cycle of time.

Moon Crescent: Transcending cycle of time.

Mark on Forehead: Renunciation of selfish and worldly desires.

Right Hand: Varda mudra showing blessing to overcome evil, and ignorance.

Alert Snake: Awareness of the laws of nature.

2 Ear Rings (Kundalayas): Represents the dual nature of creation (Shiva-Shakti, male-female, yin-yang)

Cobra: Symbolic of passion and desires.

Shiv Lingam and Yoni: Eternal procreative germ.

Rudraksha Necklace: Rudra means strict and Aksha means eye. Represents law and order in the universe

Water on Shiv Lingam: Symbolic of procreation.

Ash clad body: Burning away materialistic desires.

Cremation Ground: Mastering the cycle of birth and death.
