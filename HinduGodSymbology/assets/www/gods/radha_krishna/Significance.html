Etymology: Krishna means dark and the attractive.

 

Spiritual and Scientific Significance

Hindus believe that Krishna represents God consciousness. The Krishna avatar is synonymous with self-realization. Krishna’s teaching also relate to yoga and self-realization. Popular Beliefs

Popular Belief

Hindu’s consider Krishna to be the ninth incarnation of Vishnu. Krishna was the eighth son Devaki and Vasudeva. A prophecy stated that the eighth son of Devaki and Vasudeva will vanquish the evil ruler Kansa of Dwarka, who was the brother of Devaki

Krishna was the brains and the governing character behind the great battle of Mahabharatha. He acted as Arjun’s charioteer and gave him strategies of defeating the Kaurava army.

Krishna showed Arjuna his Vishwarupa when Arjuna was getting cold feet before the start of the war because he was going to war against his own kith and kin. Krishna explained to him that the will of time is inevitable and Arjun himself was a mere medium to facilitate it.

 

Worship

The chanting of the mantra Hare Krishna-Hare Rama is one of the most common and wide spread ways Krishna is worshipped.

 

Consorts:

Radha is considered as his first lover and friend during Krishna’s youth. She is known as the original Goddess of Shakti. Rukmini is the queen consort of Krishna who had eloped with him upon her own request as she was being forced upon a marriage. She is also known as the incarnation of Goddess Lakshmi.

 

Associated Festival:

Krishna Janmasthami is the festival associated with Krishna and is celebrated throughout India with great reverence and joy.
